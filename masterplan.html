16-Week Metabolic Reversal

Vishwanath · 34M · 101 → ~85–91 kg · reverse fatty liver + insulin resistance + build fitness
The one lever: the calorie deficit does the weight loss (no drug — so adherence is everything); high protein + evening lifting keep it fat-not-muscle; morning Zone-2 rebuilds the engine (HIIT gated behind BP + OSA clearance); a fixed 6 AM wake anchors the clock.
Expectation-set: 16 kg is realistic but the optimistic edge. A 10–13 kg result already reverses most of the syndrome — that's a win, not a miss. Adherence to the deficit is the gate, not the drug or the gym.

3 · Daily execution

Diet: ~1600–1800 kcal base days · protein 150–170 g (floor 150) · lower-carb Indian-adapted (1 katori brown rice OR 2 millet rotis at one meal) · fiber 30–35 g + psyllium · zero alcohol · <25 g added sugar, no sweet drinks · 2.5–3 L water · ~2 g sodium. No carb-heavy late meals (one caused a reactive glucose low of 50) — dinner earlier + lower-GI; CGM watches it.

Day: 6:00 wake + aripiprazole · 6:30 fasted Z2 + daylight · 11:00 meal (~45 g P) · 14:30 meal (~45 g P) · 17:00 pre-lift whey · 17:30–18:30 strength · 18:45 dinner (~50 g P, last food by 7) · patch off at bed · 22:00 sleep.
5:2 days: keep the AM walk, skip heavy lifting, keep all supplements.

4 · Training

Strength 3×/wk (home gym) — squat/hinge/press/pull/carry; 2→4 sets, RPE 5–6, reps-before-load, no Valsalva / submaximal (BP + OSA). Rising strength in a deficit = muscle preserved.

Cardio: Zone 2 by talk-test (~110–129 bpm early, conservative), 20→45 min; steps 8–12k.

HIIT — gated: only after BP <135/85 (home) AND OSA reviewed/treated, ~Wk 9–12+, 1×/wk replacing a Z2. Untreated OSA + BP makes intervals the highest-risk modality.

5 · Medications & supplements

No GLP-1, no metformin — pure lifestyle (your decision). This avoids the cost, injections and GI side effects, but removes the appetite-suppression cushion — so the deficit rides entirely on adherence (realistic 10–13 kg, 16 the stretch). The lower-GI + protein + fiber diet is what keeps hunger steady without a drug. Protein + creatine + lifting stay mandatory (muscle). Famotidine continues for reflux. Revisit a GLP-1 only if you plateau badly and choose to.

Stack: Vit D (Uprise 60K wkly ×8 → recheck, likely 3000–4000 IU/day) · plain iron + vit C (ferrous bisglycinate, no-zinc → faster repletion; reassess ferritin 8–12 wk, target 30–100) · methylcobalamin B12 1500 · magnesium 220 mg (PM) · omega-3 ~2.5 g/day · creatine 5 g · multivitamin (lunch) · psyllium · whey (the protein backstop). All audited within safe limits; iron at meal 1 away from dairy/tea/coffee/eggs + the multivit. Full timed schedule on the tracker.

6 · Sleep · OSA · nose · smoking

Sleep: problem is late bedtime, not capacity → fix the 6 AM wake + morning light; screens off 90 min pre-bed; no clonazepam/trazodone as sleep crutch.

OSA: do the study (demand AHI + SpO2 + positional). AHI ≥15 → CPAP (not insured — out of pocket); 5–14 → oral appliance (your small jaw = good responder). ~16 kg loss may cut AHI ~40% and downgrade/resolve it — re-test after the loss before committing to a device.

Nose: currently clear on Otrivin — stop it (rebound risk), switch to a steroid spray (ask doctor). With spray + weight loss, surgery is likely avoidable; it's elective, insurance-covered only after 27 Nov 2026, and reserved for if it blocks CPAP or symptoms worsen.

Smoking: hold 7 mg patch 2–4 wk → stop; lozenge PRN; smoke-free before HIIT. Nicotine inflates BP — another reason to confirm the home baseline.

7 · Monitoring & labs

Daily: weight (7-day average). Weekly: waist; home BP. Every 2 wk: body comp (trend only).
Wk 8: fasting insulin/glucose, lipids, ALT/AST/GGT, ferritin, Vit D, magnesium, hs-CRP. (HOMA-IR + TG move most; HbA1c/HDL may not — normal.)
Wk 16: full panel + testosterone/SHBG + repeat liver USG + repeat stress test + body comp.
Recheck Vit D after the 8-wk loading; ferritin at 8–12 wk. Consider FIB-4 / FibroScan — normal enzymes don't exclude NASH.
Creatine note: pause it 5–7 days before any kidney/creatinine/eGFR draw — it raises serum creatinine artificially (benign, not kidney damage).
