🪜 The progressive ladder — one rung at a time, no deadline
Graduate each rung by mastery (it feels automatic, ~8–12 wk) — not by date. Sleep is the keystone: it restores appetite hormones, willpower & cravings, so diet and quitting actually work. Fix it first.
NOWFoundation — supplements (AM: D3 · iron+vit C · B12 · omega-3 · creatine · multivit · PM: magnesium glycinate · timed: melatonin 0.5 mg) + nicotine patch 24 h. Corrects the deficiencies (D 16, ferritin 14.7, Mg) that themselves wreck sleep.
NOW · 1SLEEP — fixed wake + 14-day tracking · morning walk 20–25 min @ RPE 3–4 + outdoor light · melatonin 0.5 mg ~1 h before target bed · magnesium PM · screens/porn out of the last hour · no late/heavy carbs · coffee before 10 AM (after the walk) · (digital CBT-I optional).
2DIET + light strength — protein-first → cut processed/sugar → deficit → 2 meals + whey · 2×/wk bodyweight @ RPE 5–6. after sleep is locked.
3Build + quit — Zone-2 cardio ~110–130 bpm 2–3×/wk + resistance · focused smoking cessation (halve coffee once fully quit).
4Push — running · HIIT (≥9 METs + resting BP <135/85 — both gates now clear) · heavier lifting.
Hard rules: no moderate+ exercise within ~4–5 h of bed · one new thing at a time · iron ≥2 h from aripiprazole+magnesium · don't stack melatonin+clonazepam+magnesium unaware · timing > dose on melatonin.
Mental wind-down — process the open loops (reference, not scheduled): what delays sleep isn't the hard work itself, it's the open loops left running. Unfinished problem-solving thoughts are the single strongest predictor of delayed onset (Wicklow & Espie 2000; Wuyts 2012), and a specific plan closes the loop even when the task stays unfinished (Masicampo & Baumeister 2011). So close the loop: end the workday with a 5-min brain-dump — tomorrow's specific next action + when — then "done." A bedtime to-do list cut sleep onset ~9 min vs. dwelling on the day (Scullin RCT). Process hard feelings earlier in the day, not at bedtime.
Guardrail (reference only — not a blanket rule): no general psychosis risk here. The "time-box / keep emotional processing contained" caution only applies if cannabis returns or cigarettes spike and you're processing heavy emotions then. Otherwise, process freely.
The foundation: fix SLEEP first — it's the keystone that restores appetite hormones, willpower and cravings, so diet and quitting actually work (poor sleep blunts both). Then layer diet, then quit — one rung at a time, no deadline. (The calorie deficit still does the weight loss once you reach the diet rung; this is about *order*, not abandoning it.)
